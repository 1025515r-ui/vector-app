Assessment tools tell you what to observe.
VECTOR tells you how to make it happen.

Why VECTOR is a new category
in early childhood diagnostics

The world has Type A tools (what to assess) and Type B tools (how to interact). VECTOR introduces Type C: how to create the pedagogical situation where the skill emerges.

01
From assessing the child to assessing the teacher's impact
For the first time, the source of change is measured — not just the outcome. The teacher's pedagogical choices become visible as data.
02
Direction of development, not just a snapshot level
Traditional tools show where a child is today. VECTOR introduces a new diagnostic category: the direction and velocity of pedagogical influence over time.
03
Skill balance as a diagnostic indicator
The balance of Soft and Hard skills is measured as a single integrated indicator — a world first for preschool diagnostic systems.
04
Intentional practice, not intuitive teaching
Teachers move from intuitive delivery to evidence-based, reflective management of children's development — with real data across up to 5 diagnostic days per year.
